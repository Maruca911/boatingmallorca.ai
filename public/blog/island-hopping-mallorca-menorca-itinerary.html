Island Hopping: Mallorca to Menorca Sailing Itinerary

Published January 2026 | Category: Itineraries

The perfect multi-day sailing adventure: Mallorca to Menorca island hopping. Crystal-clear waters, unique anchorages, authentic villages, and Mediterranean culture. This 5-7 day itinerary suits intermediate to advanced sailors.

Day 1: Palma to Cabrera (35nm)

Sailing distance: 35 nautical miles | Duration: 6-7 hours | Conditions: Moderate, south-southwest winds typical

Depart Palma early morning. Sail south to Cabrera National Park. Anchor in designated mooring buoys (permit required). Crystal-clear waters, snorkeling, and pristine anchorage. Few boats overnight here due to permits.

Day 2: Cabrera to Formentera (30nm)

Sailing distance: 30nm | Duration: 5-6 hours

Head southeast to Formentera. Anchor at Playa de Illetas or Es Pujols. Small island, bohemian atmosphere, excellent beachfront restaurants. Snorkeling and beach time. Fewer tourists than Ibiza.

Day 3: Formentera to Ibiza (25nm)

Sailing distance: 25nm | Duration: 4-5 hours

Sail to Ibiza. Anchor in San Antonio Bay or near the Old Town. Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Old Town, explore restaurants, or enjoy nightlife. Busier than previous days but incredible energy.

Day 4: Ibiza to Menorca (60nm)

Sailing distance: 60nm | Duration: 9-11 hours

Longer passage to Menorca. Morning departure recommended. Anchor at Mahón harbor (east) or Ciudadela (west). Mahón offers more services; Ciudadela is more picturesque.

Day 5-6: Menorca Exploration

Menorca offers incredible anchorages: Cala Mitjana, Cala Macarella, Cala Presili. Quieter than Mallorca, excellent holding, pristine beaches. Spend 2-3 days exploring different coves.

Day 7: Return to Mallorca

Return passage to Mallorca (60nm from Menorca). Aim for Port de Pollenca or Palma depending on timing and conditions.

Essential Planning Tips

  • Season: May-October best conditions. Summer (July-August) busiest but most reliable winds
  • Permits: Cabrera National Park requires advance booking (free, limited slots)
  • Provisioning: Stock supplies in Palma before departing
  • Weather: Monitor wind forecasts. Northerly winds common in June-September afternoons
  • Reservations: Menorca anchorages fill in summer. Arrive early for best spots
  • Costs: Assume 30-40 EUR/day provisioning, 5-10 EUR/night mooring fees
