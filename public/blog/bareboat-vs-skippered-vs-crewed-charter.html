Bareboat vs Skippered vs Crewed: Which Charter Type is Right for You?

Published January 2026 | Category: Guides

Choosing the right charter type is essential for your Mallorca sailing experience. Each option serves different needs, skill levels, and budgets. Here's how they compare:

Bareboat Charter

What it is: You captain your own yacht with no crew provided. You're responsible for navigation, sailing, and all decisions.

Best for: Experienced sailors, those who want full control, budget-conscious groups

Cost: 1,500-3,000 EUR/week for 35-45ft sailboat

Requirements: ICC (International Certificate of Competence) or equivalent. Some bareboat companies require RYA Day Skipper or higher.

  • Pros: Maximum freedom, lowest cost, learn local waters intimately, create your own itinerary
  • Cons: Full responsibility for safety and vessel, requires sailing competence, long hours of active sailing

Skippered Charter

What it is: Professional captain handles navigation and sailing. You relax and assist as desired.

Best for: Those without sailing experience, first-time charters, families who want minimal stress

Cost: 2,500-5,000 EUR/week (includes skipper salary)

Requirements: None—skipper has full certification

  • Pros: Stress-free charter, learn from an expert, safe and legal compliance guaranteed, local knowledge included
  • Cons: Less autonomy, higher cost, shared itinerary with skipper's preferences

Crewed Yacht Charter (Luxury)

What it is: Full professional crew including captain, chef, stewardess, engineer. Premium service and gourmet dining.

Best for: Special occasions, luxury holidays, non-sailors who want complete premium service

Cost: 10,000-50,000+ EUR/week (depends on yacht size and season)

Requirements: Deep pockets and desire for luxury

  • Pros: Ultimate luxury, personalized service, gourmet meals, professional crew expertise, highest comfort level
  • Cons: Significantly higher cost, less intimacy/privacy, advanced bookings required

How to Choose

Choose Bareboat if: You have sailing certification, want maximum itinerary freedom, enjoy hands-on sailing, and have an experienced crew

Choose Skippered if: You want to relax, lack sailing experience, travel with non-sailors, or want local expert guidance

Choose Crewed if: Budget allows, you're celebrating a special occasion, or you desire luxury service and gourmet dining

Certification Requirements

For Bareboat: ICC certificate (2-3 days to obtain, cost 150-300 EUR) or RYA Day Skipper certification (5 days, cost 300-500 EUR)

For Skippered: No certification needed—skipper provides professional leadership

For Crewed: No certification needed—crew handles everything
