Complete Buyer's Guide: How to Purchase a Yacht in Mallorca

Published: January 2026 | Category: Guides | Author: Boating Mallorca Team

Mallorca is Europe's premier destination for yacht purchases, with a strong market, professional services, and excellent financing options. This guide walks you through the entire buying process.

Step 1: Define Your Needs and Budget

Before beginning your search, clearly define what you want: vessel type, size, age, condition, intended use, and budget range. Mallorca's market serves buyers from 50,000 EUR for a used RIB to multi-million EUR for a superyacht. Consider whether you'll use it for personal enjoyment, charter income, liveaboard living, or as an investment.

Step 2: Research the Market

Study comparable sales in your target category. Visit major yacht brokers in Palma, attend boat shows, and browse online listings on YachtWorld, eBay Motors, and Mediterranean brokers' websites. You'll develop a realistic understanding of pricing and available inventory.

Step 3: Arrange Professional Survey

This is non-negotiable. Hire an independent marine surveyor to inspect the hull, engine, systems, and structure. Cost: 2,000-4,000 EUR. A detailed survey reveals hidden issues and justifies your offer price.

Step 4: Financing and Insurance

Most banks offer yacht financing at 60-70% LTV (loan-to-value). Interest rates range from 2.5-4.5% depending on vessel age and condition. Get pre-approval before making an offer. Obtain marine insurance quotes—typical cost is 1-3% of boat value annually.

Step 5: Negotiate and Make an Offer

Start your offer 10-15% below asking price. Include survey contingencies, sea trial clauses, and repair allowances. Most sellers expect negotiation—this is normal practice.

Step 6: Complete Legal Transfer

Hire a maritime lawyer to handle title transfer, registration, and Spanish maritime law compliance. This typically costs 2,000-5,000 EUR but is essential for clean ownership and financing.

Understanding Ownership Costs

Insurance 1,000-3,000 EUR
Maintenance 5,000-10,000 EUR
Fuel (if motor) 2,000-5,000 EUR

Charter Income Potential

Many buyers invest in yachts for charter income. A well-positioned vessel in Mallorca can generate 8-12% annual yield. Some owners use revenue-sharing management companies to handle bookings and logistics.

Key Considerations

  • Mediterranean Registry: Vessels registered in Spain/Balearics command premium prices and resale value
  • Age Factor: Boats over 40 years old require additional certifications; 10-20 years is the sweet spot for buyers
  • Engine Hours: Critical for motor yachts. Low hours (under 5,000) justify higher prices
  • Recent Refits: New engines, electronics, or structural work add 12-20% to value
  • Liveaboard Status: If you plan to live aboard, ensure the vessel is certified and insured for continuous occupancy

Top Purchase Locations in Mallorca

Palma de Mallorca: Largest selection, luxury marinas, professional services. Best for serious buyers and high-value purchases.

Port d'Andratx: Exclusive clientele, premium vessels, boutique marinas. Best for luxury and high-net-worth purchases.

Port de Pollenca: Sailing community, good value, family boats. Best for experienced sailors.

Cala Ratjada: East coast hub, fishing boats, affordable day-charter vessels. Best for budget-conscious or investment-focused buyers.
