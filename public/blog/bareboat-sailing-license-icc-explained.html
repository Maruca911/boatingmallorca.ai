Bareboat Sailing License in Spain: ICC Certificate Explained

Published January 2026 | Category: Certifications

Want to charter a bareboat in Mallorca? You'll need an ICC certificate. Here's everything you need to know about obtaining this internationally recognized sailing qualification.

What is ICC?

ICC stands for International Certificate of Competence. It's recognized across EU and Mediterranean waters as proof of basic sailing competency. Required by most bareboat charter companies in Spain, Croatia, Greece, and Italy.

ICC Requirements

  • Minimum age 18 years
  • Good swimming ability (ability to swim 50m in moderate conditions)
  • Basic sailing knowledge and experience
  • Pass written exam (40 questions, 80% pass rate)
  • Practical sailing assessment (optional for some training organizations)

Duration and Cost

Intensive course: 2-3 days | Cost: 150-300 EUR

Online + exam: 1 day in-person exam | Cost: 100-150 EUR

Existing sailors: Written exam only (1 day) | Cost: 50-100 EUR

Training Locations in Mallorca

Multiple sailing schools in Palma, Port de Pollenca, and Cala Ratjada offer ICC training. Most charter companies also recommend training partners.

ICC Exam Content

  • Sailing theory (knots, rigging, sail trim)
  • Navigation and chart work
  • Weather and tide understanding
  • Safety procedures and equipment
  • International Rules of the Road (collision avoidance)
  • Communication (VHF radio procedure)

Alternative Certifications

RYA Day Skipper: More comprehensive, 5-day course. Cost 300-500 EUR. Preferred by some charter companies for advanced skills.

RYA Coastal Skipper: Advanced 5-day course. Cost 400-600 EUR. Recommended for ambitious sailing projects.

After Certification

  • ICC valid for 10 years
  • Renewal requires refresher course (1-2 days)
  • Valid across entire EU and Mediterranean
  • Charter companies may require additional sea trial assessment on their boats

Is ICC Worth It?

Yes if: You plan multiple bareboat charters, want genuine sailing skills, need legal compliance.

Alternative: Hire a skipper on your first few charters while learning. Transition to bareboat once comfortable.
