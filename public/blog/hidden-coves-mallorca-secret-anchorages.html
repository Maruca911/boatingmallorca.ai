Hidden Coves of Mallorca: Secret Anchorages Guide

Published January 2026 | Category: Destinations

Mallorca's most memorable boating moments happen at hidden coves unreachable by car. These secret anchorages offer crystal-clear waters, privacy, and authentic Mediterranean beauty. Here are the best-kept secrets.

Northwest Coast Coves

Sa Calobra

A dramatic canyon opening into the sea near Soller. The steep cliff backdrop and deep anchorage create an unforgettable scene. This is Mallorca's most iconic anchorage but worth the navigation challenge.

Best for: Experienced sailors | Depth: 15-30m | Skill Level: Advanced

Cala Deia

Intimate cove near the artist village of Deia. Small beach, excellent seafood restaurants within walking distance, perfect for swimming. Charming and less crowded than major anchorages.

Best for: Romantic getaways | Depth: 8-12m | Skill Level: Intermediate

South Coast Hidden Gems

Cala Mondragó

Protected cove with white sand beach and clear waters. Part of a natural park, so visitor numbers are limited. Pristine and family-friendly with good holding.

Best for: Families | Depth: 5-12m | Skill Level: Beginner

Es Trenc

Four-kilometer stretch of sandy beach with minimal development. Crystal-clear water, wide beach for swimming and beach clubs. Popular but never overcrowded due to its length.

Best for: Day trips | Depth: 2-8m | Skill Level: Beginner

East Coast Secrets

Cala Varques

Sandy cove accessed only by boat or long hike. Turquoise water, soft sand, dramatic cliffs backdrop. One of Mallorca's most pristine and peaceful anchorages.

Best for: Privacy seekers | Depth: 6-15m | Skill Level: Intermediate

Cala Mago

Near Andratx on the west coast. Strong holding in sand, protected from most winds, excellent for overnight stays. Less crowded than Palma bay anchorages.

Best for: Overnight anchorage | Depth: 8-18m | Skill Level: Intermediate

Cabrera Archipelago (National Park)

South of Mallorca, Cabrera National Park offers the most pristine Mediterranean waters. Permit required; overnight stays limited. The Blue Grotto (Cova Blava) is unmissable. Best for experienced sailors with advance planning.

Practical Tips for Exploring Hidden Coves

  • Visit in shoulder season (May-June, September-October) for fewer crowds and better weather
  • Check weather and holding ground conditions before anchoring
  • Carry detailed charts—some coves have unmarked rocks and shallow sections
  • Use GPS coordinates for precise navigation to smaller coves
  • Bring tender or dinghy to explore shallow areas safely
  • Respect environmental protection zones; some coves have restrictions
