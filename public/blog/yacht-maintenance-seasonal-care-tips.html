Yacht Maintenance Checklist: Seasonal Care Tips

Published January 2026 | Category: Maintenance

Regular maintenance is the difference between a reliable vessel and a money pit. Mallorca's Mediterranean climate—sun exposure, salt water, and seasonal usage patterns—creates specific maintenance demands. Here's your complete seasonal guide.

Spring Prep (March-April)

  • Full engine service: oil, filters, impeller, zincs
  • Check fuel system and filters
  • Inspect all through-hulls and seacocks
  • Test battery and charging system
  • Review safety equipment (flares, first aid)
  • Inspect hull for cracks or osmotic blistering
  • Clean and lubricate all deck hardware
  • Check rigging tension (for sailing vessels)

Summer Usage (May-September)

  • Monthly engine hour tracking
  • Weekly visual inspections before sailing
  • Regular fuel stabilizer if not in constant use
  • UV protection: regular wax/sealant to prevent fading
  • Monthly cooling system check (engine temp monitoring)
  • Clean sea strainers after each use
  • Inspect through-hulls monthly

Fall/Autumn (September-October)

  • Full propulsion system inspection
  • Test gearbox/transmission oil condition
  • Inspect rudder and steering
  • Clean and protect all electronics
  • Deep hull cleaning to prevent growth
  • Check through-hulls and seals

Winter Storage (October-March)

Engine Winterization

  • Change oil and filters one final time
  • Stabilize fuel for long-term storage
  • Drain water cooling systems
  • Fog engine with fogging oil
  • Cover engine intake

Hull and Systems

  • Remove and store batteries or use trickle charger
  • Close through-hulls and drain lines
  • Cover boat to prevent UV and moisture damage
  • Maintain proper ventilation to prevent mold
  • Inspect for rodent/pest entry points

Critical Maintenance Items (Every 5 Years)

  • Haul-out: Bottom cleaning, inspection, antifouling recoat (3,000-8,000 EUR)
  • Professional survey: Structural inspection (2,000-4,000 EUR)
  • Gel coat repairs: Address cracks and oxidation
  • Electrical system overhaul: Replace corroded fittings
  • Through-hull replacement: Seals and fittings

Cost-Saving Tips

  • DIY basic maintenance: oil changes, filter replacement, cleaning
  • Join boating clubs for discounted maintenance partnerships
  • Negotiate annual service contracts with local boatyards (10-15% savings)
  • Track maintenance meticulously for warranty claims
  • Preventive maintenance costs far less than emergency repairs
