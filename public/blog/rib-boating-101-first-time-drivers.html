RIB Boating 101: Everything First-Time RIB Drivers Need to Know

Published January 2026 | Category: Beginners

RIBs (Rigid Inflatable Boats) are exhilarating, accessible, and perfect for exploring Mallorca's coastline. Here's everything new drivers need to know.

What is a RIB?

RIBs combine rigid hulls with inflatable tubes. They're highly maneuverable, stable, fast, and forgiving of beginner errors. Sizes range 5m-12m. Perfect for day trips, water sports, and exploring coves.

Key Differences from Sailboats

  • No sailing experience required: Just point and accelerate
  • Immediate response: Steering and throttle control are instant
  • Speed: 25-40+ knots possible (vs 6-12 knots sailing)
  • Noise: Engine is loud; communication requires hand signals or radio
  • Fuel consumption: 30-60 liters/hour depending on speed

Pre-Departure Safety Checklist

  • Check fuel level (never below half-tank)
  • Inspect tubes for punctures or damage
  • Verify all passengers wear USCG-approved life jackets
  • Check weather and wind forecasts
  • File float plan with someone ashore
  • Test engine start and throttle response
  • Verify VHF radio functionality

Operating a RIB

Starting: Turn key, check gauges, engage gently. Let engine warm 2-3 minutes.

Acceleration: Slow, smooth throttle increase. Abrupt acceleration can flip lighter RIBs or throw passengers.

Turning: RIBs turn tightly. Reduce speed before hard turns to prevent rolling.

Stopping: Shift to neutral first, then reverse briefly if needed. Never jam into reverse at high speed.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Operating with insufficient fuel—always know fuel consumption rate
  • Excessive speed in shallow areas or near swimmers
  • Ignoring weather changes quickly
  • Failing to adjust for passenger weight distribution
  • Not maintaining safe distance from other vessels
  • Operating outside personal skill level

Weather and Water Conditions

  • Chop: Small waves 0.5-1m manageable but uncomfortable
  • Swells: Long period waves (1.5m+) require careful navigation
  • Current: Use depth sounder and GPS to monitor position
  • Wind: Accelerates as you move away from shore. Check wind forecast before departing

RIB Maintenance

  • After each use: Rinse with fresh water, check tubes for damage
  • Monthly: Inspect engine, check oil, verify fittings tightness
  • Seasonally: Professional engine service, tube pressure checks
  • Storage: Keep in shade when not in use; UV degrades tubes

Navigation Tips

  • Use GPS and chart plotter when available
  • Stay 300m+ from swimmers and diving areas
  • Maintain 30+ knot speed in designated areas only
  • Never operate at night without proper lighting
  • Know local regulations for speed and restricted zones

Have More Fun & Stay Safe

Take a boating safety course. Many provide hands-on RIB experience. Cost: 100-300 EUR. Highly recommended for first-time drivers.
