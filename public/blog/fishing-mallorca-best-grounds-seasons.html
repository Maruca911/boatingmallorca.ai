Fishing in Mallorca: Best Grounds, Species & Seasons

Published January 2026 | Category: Fishing

Mallorca offers world-class fishing opportunities. From deep-sea game fish to coastal species, the island's Mediterranean waters support diverse species. Here's your complete fishing guide.

Top Fishing Ports

Cala Ratjada (East Coast): Premier fishing hub. Charter boats depart daily. Excellent for bluefin tuna, amberjack, dorado. Best June-September.

Port d'Alcudia (North): Sheltered bay with good fishing. Charter availability year-round. Good for mixed species.

Palma (South): Deep water fishing grounds nearby. Access to blue-water species. Multiple charter companies.

Species & Seasons

Species Best Months Weight Range Difficulty
Bluefin Tuna June-September 200-500+ kg Extreme
Swordfish July-September 50-300 kg Very High
Dorado (Mahi-Mahi) May-October 5-40 kg Moderate
Amberjack April-November 10-50 kg Moderate
Grouper Year-round 2-15 kg Easy-Moderate

Charter Fishing Costs

Half-day charter: 400-600 EUR (4 hours, up to 4 people)

Full-day charter: 800-1,200 EUR (8 hours, up to 6 people)

Big game charter: 1,500-3,000 EUR (targeting tuna/swordfish)

Includes: boat, captain, crew, tackle, ice. You supply your own fishing license (usually included).

Expert Tips

  • Book charters in advance during peak season (July-August)
  • Early morning departures yield better conditions and catches
  • Summer thermal winds complicate afternoon fishing
  • Expect seasickness in deep water—take preventive medication
  • Bring sun protection: hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
  • Consider a fishing guide if this is your first Mediterranean experience
